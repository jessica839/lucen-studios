Our Story

We didn't learn this
in a boardroom.

From a chance meeting on a dive boat in Bali to running a five-star resort in Belize, managing operations across four continents, and building AI systems for small businesses — this is how we got here.

Fifteen years of
real operations.

Most consultants tell you how to run a business. We've actually run them — in a dozen countries, in three languages, under pressure. That's what makes us different. Not our credentials. Our calluses.

2005–2009
Silicon Valley, USA

Jessica starts in software.

Before the dive boats and the cave systems, there was Silicon Valley. Jessica spent four years in software sales — learning how technology gets sold, how businesses make buying decisions, and what separates a product that sticks from one that doesn't. It's a foundation she still draws on every day.

2009
Bali, Indonesia

She trades the office for the ocean.

In 2009, Jessica left Silicon Valley to travel — and ended up in Bali doing her Divemaster training. It was there she met Matthias. Shortly after, Matthias moved to Thailand to begin his own Divemaster training. Jessica followed. A shared passion for the underwater world — and for each other — turned out to be enough.

2009–2010
Thailand

Divemasters. First season together.

Working side by side as divemasters in Thailand, we ran briefings, managed guests from a dozen different countries, and learned what it actually takes to deliver a flawless experience under pressure. The dive industry is unforgiving of poor operations. We learned fast.

2010
Florida Keys, USA

PADI Instructor certification.

Both completed the PADI Instructor Development Course in the Florida Keys — one of the most demanding training environments in the world. Running courses, managing equipment, coordinating logistics, keeping guests safe. Every day, without margin for error.

2010
Egypt

Instructing in the Red Sea.

From the Florida Keys straight to Egypt. Teaching in the Red Sea meant adapting fast — different guests, different conditions, different cultural context. The operational lessons kept stacking up.

Late 2010
Koh Phi Phi, Thailand

First dive operation management.

Back to Thailand — this time in a management role, running a dive operation on Koh Phi Phi. Scheduling, staffing, equipment, safety systems, supplier relationships. We built SOPs from scratch because there were none. We found out exactly how much time bad processes cost, and exactly how much good ones save.

2011
Honduras

IDC Staff Instructor.

One of the highest certifications in recreational diving — focused not on teaching divers, but on training instructors. Precision, communication, and the ability to transfer complex skills under pressure. We brought that standard to everything that followed.

2011–2012
Mexico · Canada

Mexico, then a remote dive lodge in Canada.

A stint instructing in Mexico, then a remote dive lodge in Canada — as different as operations get. One was high-volume and fast-paced; the other was wilderness logistics in cold water. Both taught us how to adapt systems to context rather than forcing context into systems.

2012
Corsica · Thailand

Cruise directors on the liveaboards.

Corsica, then back to Thailand as cruise directors on liveaboard dive vessels — some of the most logistically complex dive operations in the world. Multi-day expeditions in open water, responsible for the full guest experience from departure to return. No margin for error. No way to call for backup.

2013
Mexico

We got married. Then went cave diving.

In 2013 we got married and headed to Mexico. For the honeymoon, Matthias suggested completing the TDI Full Cave Diver certification — the most technically demanding dive course in the technical diving world.

"Full cave diving was not what Jessica had in mind for a honeymoon. I maintain it was a perfectly romantic choice. She disagrees. We are still married, which I consider a win." — Matthias
2013–2014
Belize

Managing a 5-star resort.

From Mexico to Belize to manage a five-star dive resort. Every touchpoint of the guest experience — from first enquiry to final review. Staff coordination, inventory, guest communications, quality control. At this level, operational failure has a very direct cost. We didn't fail.

2015
Mexico

Work permit approved. Our own business.

After years of building systems for other people's operations, we finally got to build one for ourselves. Work permit approved, we moved back to Mexico and opened our own dive operation — building everything from scratch the way we'd always known it should be done.

2016
Mexico

Our daughter arrives. Jessica returns to SaaS.

Our daughter was born in 2016. Jessica returned to the software world — bringing her Silicon Valley foundation and seven years of field operations back into SaaS sales. The perspective was different this time. She knew exactly where the technology fell short, and exactly where it could deliver.

2018
Europe

Back to Europe. Matthias joins SaaS.

We moved back to Europe for our daughter's education. Matthias transitioned into software — joining companies including PayFit and Beekeeper in Zurich in sales and account management. Both of us were now in SaaS, watching the same operational problems we'd fixed manually in dive centers repeat themselves in small businesses everywhere — only now with bigger budgets and software that still only partially solved them.

2023
Lucerne, Switzerland

Jessica moves into Marketing & Communications.

Jessica transitioned into Marketing and Communications for a company specialising in synthetic fiber treatment for industrial applications. A long way from dive boats — but the same core challenge: communicating complex value clearly, to the right audience, at the right moment. The answer increasingly involved AI.

2026
Lucerne, Switzerland

Gamper Klimmek Consulting.

We founded Gamper Klimmek Consulting to bring everything together. Silicon Valley software sales. Dive operations across four continents. SaaS sales and account management. AI implementation. Two people who have run real businesses under real pressure — and know exactly what it takes to make them work better.
